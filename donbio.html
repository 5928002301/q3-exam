Early Life

Caleb Zackery Toliver, known professionally as Don Toliver, grew up in Houston, Texas. Influenced by artists like Kanye West, Kid Cudi, and Travis Scott, Toliver developed a melodic and atmospheric sound that blends hip-hop, R&B, and trap elements.

Rise to Fame

Don Toliver gained widespread recognition with his feature on Travis Scott's song "CAN'T SAY" from the album Astroworld (2018). His breakthrough came with the release of his debut studio album, Heaven or Hell (2020), which featured hits like "No Idea" and "After Party." His distinct vocals and smooth production style quickly set him apart in the music industry.

Major Albums and Success

  • Heaven or Hell (2020): Featuring hits like "No Idea" and "After Party," this album established Toliver as a rising star.
  • Life of a Don (2021): A critically acclaimed album with songs like "Way Bigger" and "Flocky Flocky" (feat. Travis Scott).
  • Love Sick (2023): Showcasing his continued evolution, this album features collaborations with artists such as Future, Justin Bieber, and Wizkid.
  • Hardstone Psycho (2024): A highly anticipated release featuring collaborations with top artists like Travis Scott and Lil Uzi Vert, further cementing his place in hip-hop.

Influence and Legacy

Don Toliver is known for his signature melodic flow, unique vocal inflections, and atmospheric production. His ability to blend rap and R&B elements has earned him a dedicated fanbase and respect from peers in the industry. With multiple chart-topping hits and a strong artistic vision, he continues to push the boundaries of modern hip-hop.
