Biography of Lil Uzi Vert

Full Name: Symere Bysil Woods

Born: July 31, 1995

Birthplace: Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, USA

Years Active: 2013–present

Early Life

Symere Woods, known professionally as Lil Uzi Vert, grew up in North Philadelphia. Influenced by artists like Marilyn Manson, Kanye West, and Wiz Khalifa, Uzi developed a unique style that blends rapid-fire flows with melodic, futuristic beats.

Rise to Fame

Lil Uzi Vert first gained attention with his debut mixtape Luv Is Rage (2015), which led to a recording contract with Atlantic Records. His breakout moment came with the 2016 mixtape Lil Uzi Vert vs. the World, featuring the hit song "Money Longer." His energetic performances and distinct fashion style quickly set him apart in the hip-hop industry.

Major Albums and Success

  • Luv Is Rage 2 (2017): Featuring the massive hit "XO TOUR Llif3," this album debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard 200.
  • Eternal Atake (2020): A long-awaited album that also debuted at No. 1, displaying Uzi's experimental and space-themed aesthetic.
  • Pink Tape (2023): Continuing his evolution, this album further cements Uzi’s status as a genre-defying artist.

Influence and Legacy

Lil Uzi Vert is known for his eclectic style, genre-blending music, and eccentric personality. He has influenced a new wave of rappers who embrace rock and punk aesthetics in hip-hop. With a loyal fanbase and numerous platinum hits, Uzi remains a dominant force in the music industry.
